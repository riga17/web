Ambulance Services

An ambulance is a specially equipped vehicle designed to provide pre-hospital care and transport patients to medical facilities during emergencies. Far more than a simple transport van, modern ambulances function as "hospitals on wheels," staffed by highly trained paramedics and EMTs who can perform life-saving interventions such as CPR, and airway management while in transit.

Emergency Rooms

Emergency rooms serve as the critical frontline of the healthcare system, operating 24/7 to provide immediate medical intervention for life-threatening conditions. Unlike standard clinics, these departments utilize a system called triage, where medical staff prioritize patients based on the severity of their illness or injury rather than their arrival time.
